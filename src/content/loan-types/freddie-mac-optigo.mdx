## What is a Freddie Mac Optigo multifamily loan?

A Freddie Mac Optigo multifamily loan finances stabilized US apartment buildings with five or more units through Freddie Mac’s Optigo lender network. Lenders size proceeds using normalized NOI with DSCR, debt yield, and LTV—core commercial multifamily metrics with Freddie-specific grids and product options.

## Where Freddie Mac Optigo fits

Optigo fits stabilized cash-flow assets and longer hold periods where agency pricing and non-recourse structures matter more than bridge speed. Evaluate alongside [Fannie Mae agency stabilized](/loan-types/agency-stabilized/), [CMBS](/loan-types/cmbs-stabilized/), and [bridge](/loan-types/bridge-value-add/) so trade-offs are explicit before term sheet selection.

A practical starting point is typical leverage in the 60-75% range and an intended hold period around 5-10 years. Actual outcomes depend on DSCR, debt yield, property quality, sponsorship depth, and market liquidity at execution.

## Core underwriting criteria

Lenders generally focus on four areas:

1. Cash-flow durability supported by normalized NOI and rent-roll integrity.
2. Sponsor experience with similar asset and loan sizes.
3. Market liquidity and refinance optionality.
4. Business-plan credibility, including expense control and capital planning.

For near-stabilized assets, document remaining lease-up or light rehab clearly. For fully stabilized strategies, emphasize rent durability and downside DSCR/debt-yield headroom.

## Advantages and constraints

### Potential advantages

- Competitive agency pricing on core and core-plus multifamily.
- Longer terms and non-recourse structures for many qualified borrowers.
- Useful alternative quote path alongside Fannie Mae for the same asset.

### Common constraints

- Hard underwriting floors on DSCR, debt yield, and leverage.
- Prepayment structures (yield maintenance or defeasance) that affect exit flexibility.
- Greater scrutiny when NOI depends on near-term rent growth or expense cuts.

## Structuring recommendations

Model Optigo under base and downside cases before lender selection. Include rate movement scenarios, occupancy stress, and expense pressure. When comparing Fannie vs Freddie, evaluate not only coupon and proceeds but also prepay, reserves, recourse framework, and timeline certainty.

## Post-close operating playbook

Track occupancy, collections, expense variance, and covenant headroom monthly. Prepare lender-quality variance commentary to reduce friction on future refinances or supplemental financing.

## Quick checklist before requesting quotes

- Finalize normalized NOI assumptions with T12 and rent-roll support.
- Test debt sizing under DSCR, debt yield, and LTV constraints.
- Confirm SPE and guarantor structure early.
- Prepare a complete diligence package before submission.
- Quote Fannie and Freddie in parallel when the asset is agency-eligible.
