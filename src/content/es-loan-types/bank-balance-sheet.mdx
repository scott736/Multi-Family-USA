## Where Bank Balance-Sheet Multifamily Loan fits in a multifamily capital strategy

Bank Balance-Sheet Multifamily Loan is typically used when sponsors need a specific combination of proceeds, pricing, flexibility, and execution certainty. In most transactions, this product should be evaluated against at least one alternative structure so trade-offs are explicit before term sheet selection.

A practical starting point is typical leverage in the 55-70% range and an intended hold period around 3-7 years. Actual outcomes depend on DSCR, debt yield, property quality, sponsorship depth, and market liquidity at the time of execution.

## Core underwriting criteria

Lenders generally focus on four areas:

1. Cash-flow durability supported by normalized NOI assumptions.
2. Sponsor experience with similar asset and strategy types.
3. Market liquidity and refinance optionality.
4. Business-plan credibility, including capex pacing and timeline realism.

For transitional strategies, include milestone-based performance targets and reserve planning. For stabilized strategies, emphasize rent durability and expense control.

## Advantages and constraints

### Potential advantages

- Product-specific alignment with certain hold and risk profiles.
- Potentially competitive structure when assumptions are well supported.
- Useful flexibility for sponsors with clear execution discipline.

### Common constraints

- Hard underwriting floors on DSCR, debt yield, and leverage.
- Covenant and reporting requirements that continue post-close.
- Greater scrutiny when assumptions depend heavily on near-term performance changes.

## Structuring recommendations

Model this loan type under base and downside cases before lender selection. Include rate movement scenarios, occupancy stress, and expense pressure. If floating-rate exposure is involved, add cap strategy and extension economics directly into your model.

When negotiating terms, compare not only coupon and proceeds but also recourse framework, reserve requirements, prepayment constraints, reporting obligations, and timeline certainty.

## Post-close operating playbook

Financing outcomes are protected through operations. Track occupancy, collections, expense variance, and covenant headroom monthly. Prepare lender-quality variance commentary to reduce friction and keep options open for extensions or refinances.

Borrowers who treat debt execution as a full-cycle process, not a one-time closing event, usually preserve more strategic flexibility.

## Quick checklist before requesting quotes

- Finalize normalized NOI assumptions with support.
- Test debt sizing under multiple lender constraints.
- Confirm legal and guarantor structure early.
- Prepare a complete diligence package before submission.
- Define post-close reporting cadence and ownership.
